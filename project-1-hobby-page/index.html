Into the Mind of Light Yagami

Who is Light Yagami? A light Overview

In this web page, I'll be analysing the protagonist/anti-hero of the Anime series, Death Note. Light Yagami was an honourary student ranked top of all his classes in Tokyo, Japan and the son of a member of the Japanese Task Force. In the year 2006, he found the mysterious "Death Note" - a book that claimed to take the life of anyone whose name is written - which fell from the sky by a "death God" known as "Ryuk".

Not only is he academically gifted, but also shows himself to be highly perceptive with a set of amazing problem-solving skills - he was praised and respected highly amongst his family and peers which laid the foundation for an intense game of Cat and Mouse with the Japanese Law Enforcement.

He will later be referred to as Kira (Killer) or Kami (God ) - a testament to the difference in perception between the public and the Law Enforcement as well as showing the viewer/reader how Light himself perceived his own character. I will be exploring both of these names to describe his sense of justice and how the consequences of his actions align with how "justice" is portrayed in the Anime Series.

The "Justice" Ideology of Light Yagami

What is the Death Note?

Before discussing the mindset of Light, we first have to define what the Death Note is. The Death Note is a book created in another world by Shinigami (Gods), these Shinigami feed off of the souls of human beings. The black book of Death was written by a Shinigami named "Ryuk" (meaning 'boring' or 'boredom' in Japanese) before dropping the book into the human world. The book we see Light using is actually a second Death Note which Ryuk wrote rules into for humans to understand - his motivations for this? Well, it's all in the meaning of his name; pure boredom which speaks volumes to the sadistic nature of the book itself, the owner and those who eventually end up using it. Who said death is another part of life? - not this Shinigami.

Rules of the Death Note:

  • "The human whose name is written in this note shall die."
  • "This note will not take effect unless the writer has the person's face in their mind when writing his/her name. Therefore, people sharing the same name will not be affected."
  • "If the cause of death is written within the next 40 seconds of writing the person's name, it will happen."
  • "If the cause of death is not specified, the person will simply die of a heart attack."
  • "After writing the cause of death, details of the death should be written in the next 6 minutes and 40 seconds."

The first encounter

Light first encounters the Death Note while in a class he notices an object flying from the sky, we also glimpse into his character during this moment - him being the only one to notice the object, they also show his intellectual prowess by suddenly snapping out his trance of wondering what the object was and answering his teacher's question which as the viewer/reader do not know what is being asked. After his class, he picks the book up and shrugs it off as some sort of sick joke, but curiosity gets the better of him.

A book that kills people by writing their name down with a mental image of the person in mind sounds absurd, it defies all logic - so what's the harm in using it? It's not as if it'll actually work is it? If he is going to use it, who would be the best person to test it on? The world of Death Note is similar to our reality, it's filled with both good and evil, but the Death Note setting is a world where there's more evil than good - there isn't enough justice, murderers serve small time, judges are paid off, media hosts scam the public with lies for profit.

Light believed he was the example of the perfect person. He was polite, highly intelligent, emotionally aware and treated in high regard by the majority, however, had a strong disdain for those who committed heinous acts against the innocent; if only someone had the power to do something about it, to have the courage to step forth, have the right amount of intellect to deal with these situations. In the world of Death Note, this is apparent in the opening scene where we see a major rise in crime on the billboard - this created his sense of justice and he now has the potential means to carry it out.

considering using the death note

In this section of the manga, we see Light's amazing intellectual prowess in action, despite the idea of using a book to take a life, he meticulously considers the circumstances on the off chance that this book actually does as it suggests; “I need to be able to know if they immediately died…”, “It should be someone not connected to me… someone whose death doesn't matter” - at this point in time he still holds onto his humanity by later coming to question why he is even taking this serious.

finding a test subject

During his train of thought, a program is interrupted by the breaking news of a hostage situation taking place at a preschool with a teacher and 7 children - the man in question is a 42 years old unemployed man with a track record of 6 kills to his name - immediately this answered Light's question and he wrote his name down… 40 seconds passed by… no death… but to his surprise the hostages were suddenly leaving the building and it was reported that the suspect died of a heart attack.

As we already know, Light is very intelligent with a radical sense of justice, one test is not enough - it could've been a coincidence, the suspects said he suddenly collapsed. It was at this moment that he appointed himself the responsibility of “cleansing the world” - he doesn't question the morality of taking another human's life but questions the validity of his test - if he could find another test subject this will be a win for him… no, for the world. To him, this man was nothing more than just scum of the earth who deserved to die and so he set out to find another.

the greater of the 2 evils

Light's confirmaiton of the Death Note's power comes from his next victim, a fellow classmate, 'Shibutaku'. Shubutaku is your typical high school bully in visual media during the 2000s - arrogant, abusive by nature and looks over 30 for some reason. We see Shibutaku in class with his friends bullying another classmate, Light acknowledges it and debates whether or not to kill him. He asks himself if it should be someone close to him to avoid trouble but mainly because he still has some of his humanity left - he questions their malicious ways and if the world would really be a better place without them.

On his way home, he decides to stop at a local gas station to get some snacks before "studying", the bullies are also there causing trouble, Shibutaku sees a young lady and prompts her to go out with him despite her saying no repeatedly - who will stop them? Well here's the perfect test sample, who will care if these pieces of filth are removed from the earth? And so he uses it - after 40 seconds, a truck strikes down Shibuktaku.

consequences

the consequences of power

Light meets Ryuk after discovering the power of the death note, his first appearance is in alliance with the book - a subtle yet dark presence. As any person would, Light is startled by his appearance - a tall dark demonic figure with red pupils who can only be seen by those who've touched the book before. Ryuk chillingly informs Light that since he has used the book as a consequence he will inevitably die by his own hands and is not sent to either Heaven or Hell for being a human user. To Ryuk's surprise, Light had already accepted death and wanted to ask him questions about using the book. Light treats his role as some sort of chosen being who will become a martyr once he's “cleansed” the earth of its evil. Ryuk funnily retorts by telling Light he only dropped the book because he was bored.

In this moment we get a glimpse into the God's complex shown through Light. After being informed that he only found the book by chance, he laughs hysterically, Ryuk confused by his reaction asks him why he even wants to become the God of Earth and points out the number of names he's already written, Light replies “Because I'm also bored”. A God stands above all and in this case, Light even wants to be beyond the creator of the book itself by not allowing Ryuk to demean his vision as a variant of luck or chance - it is fate - and by fate, he claims that the book belongs to Earth itself because it's landed here now.

"l" the antagonist

the adversary

Light's adversary is a special investigator called “L”. He represents the opposition to Light's philosophy of cleansing the world - he is also very intelligent and experienced with high-profile investigations that the Japanese government cannot handle. He is such an asset to the task force that no one has ever seen his face before, instead, he communicates through his right-hand man, Watari. L differs in personality however coming across as an antisocial weirdo with childish tendencies but it's not until we see his first appearance that we find out his true prowess.

With a number of seemingly “natural” deaths happening in Japan, the task force could not figure out a way to find out why or who is performing these murders so they resort to calling L to investigate.

Finally, the Cat and Mouse chase begins with L making his debut in the case by demonstrating his experience and intellect. He instructs Watari to have a fake “L” make an appearance on television claiming he will take down this vigilante once and for all to which Light responds by promptly writing the person's name down and he dies after 40 seconds… without any hesitation, L's signature appears on the screen informing the killer that this was a fake and performs incredible speculation on a potential lead by deducing the events of the recent murders and what's just happened:
